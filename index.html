Problem Statement

As damaging events linked to climate change become more visible and potentially more frequent, those seen as contributing to greenhouse gas emissions could face increasing moral and financial liability. Responding to these pressures will require meaningful, quantifiable action to reduce reliance on energy generated from fossil fuels.

Those engaged in performance-intensive computing applications such as high-end gaming will be among many consumers in need of data and analytics to empower them to align consumption patterns with climate priorities.

Problem Background

A high-end gaming rig could consume as much as 1,100 kilowatt hours per year and account for as much as 2,000 pounds of CO2 annually, with regional fuel mix a major determining factor in emissions.

Despite making up just 2.5% of personal computers, estimates found that in 2012 gaming PCs consumed around 20% of all electricity usage across personal desktop, notebook, and console devices combined.

Gamers generally not made aware of concrete steps they could take to reduce the energy demands of their computing habits.
